Game reviews are one of the most common types of videogame journalism, as well as one of the most common forms of game debate. How can we define them and gain a better understanding of the role they can play in forming and shaping our perceptions of games? The findings of a study that evaluated and deconstructed a collection of game reviews from prominent online websites are presented in this article. Our findings show that game reviews cover a wide range of themes and subjects, and that treating them solely as "shopping guides" overlooks their larger role in the game discourse. We discovered that game design is often mentioned in game reviews. We discovered that game reviews often contain game design recommendations, speculate on the motives and ambitions of game developers, and provide readers with tips on how to approach and enjoy specific games. Game reviews, in general, will aid in the preservation of videogame culture by contextualizing the similarities and historical associations between games.
